ECONOMY SYSTEM

17 terms
Money Concept
/economy balance
Money is the universal virtual currency in Astra. Every user has their own money balance that is shared across all servers (it is not server-specific). You earn money through daily rewards, working, mining, gambling, and passive income items. Money is used to buy items in the server shop, trade stocks, and participate in the economy.
See also Cash Bank Balance
Balance Concept
/economy balance
Your balance is the total amount of money you own. It is split into two parts: Cash (money in your pocket, exposed to robbery) and Bank (money deposited safely). The balance command shows both values, your total net worth, and any items with passive income. Your balance is global — the same in every server.
/economy balance → shows cash, bank, and total
Cash Concept
On-hand money
Cash is money you are carrying "on your person." It is the amount at risk if another user successfully robs you. When you earn money (from daily, work, gambling wins, etc.), it goes into cash by default. You can protect cash by depositing it into your bank. Cash is also what you spend when buying items in the shop.
See also Bank Rob Deposit
Bank Concept
/economy deposit · /economy withdraw
The bank is a safe storage vault for your money. Money stored in the bank cannot be stolen by other users using the rob command. There is no interest or limit on how much you can bank. Use /economy deposit <amount> to move cash to the bank, and /economy withdraw <amount> to take it back out. Strategic banking is important if you play in competitive servers where robbery is common.
/economy deposit 5000 → moves 5,000 from cash to bank
Daily Reward Command
/economy daily · -daily (DMs)
The daily reward lets you claim free money once every 24 hours. The base reward grows larger the longer your streak is — claim every day without missing to maximize your earnings. Missing a day resets your streak back to 1. The reward is added directly to your cash. You can use this command in DMs too.
/economy daily → claims +2,600 money (🔥 3-day streak)
See also Streak
Streak Concept
Consecutive daily claims
Your streak counts how many consecutive days you have claimed your daily reward without missing a day. A higher streak gives you a larger daily payout — the formula rewards consistent players. If you miss a full 24-hour window from your last claim time, your streak resets to 1. Your current streak is displayed every time you run the daily command.
Work Command
/economy work
The work command simulates doing a job to earn money. It has a cooldown (typically 1–2 hours) so you cannot spam it. Each time you work, you receive a random amount of money within a set range. Working is one of the safest ways to build up your cash balance without risking anything. Results and flavor text vary — it simulates different professions each time.
/economy work → earned 1,250 money as a "coder"
Harvest Command
/economy harvest
Harvest collects accumulated passive income from items you own in the server shop. Items like farms, mines, and businesses generate money over time — but that money sits uncollected until you run harvest. There is a cooldown between harvests. The more passive income items you own, the larger your harvest will be. This makes shopping strategic: buying items that produce recurring income pays off over time.
See also Passive Income Shop
Passive Income Concept
Automatic earnings over time
Passive income is money that accumulates automatically from items you have purchased in the shop. You do not need to do anything — the money builds up in the background. To collect it, use /economy harvest. Items with passive income are usually more expensive upfront but pay for themselves over time. The rate at which money accumulates depends on the specific item purchased.
Rob Command
/economy rob @user
Rob lets you attempt to steal money from another user's cash (not their bank). The attempt has a chance to succeed or fail — if it fails, you pay a fine instead. Only cash is at risk; banked money is completely safe. You cannot rob users with very little cash. There is a cooldown between rob attempts. This adds a risk vs. reward element to the economy — keep your cash banked if you don't want to lose it.
/economy rob @alice → stole 400 money (or: failed, lost 200)
Gamble Command
/economy gamble <amount>
The gamble command lets you wager a specified amount of your cash on a random outcome. You can win or lose your bet — the house edge is slight. Gambling is high-risk, high-reward. Only your cash is at risk (not banked money). There is a minimum and maximum bet amount. For a fun experience, pair it with strategic banking to limit your exposure.
Slots Command
/economy slots <bet>
The slot machine mini-game. You place a bet and three spinning reels land on random symbols. Matching all three symbols pays out a big multiplier. Two matching symbols pays a smaller amount. No match means you lose your bet. The payout varies by symbol — rarer symbols pay more. This command has a cooldown between spins.
/economy slots 500 → 🍋 🍋 🍋 — WIN! +1,500 money
Coinflip Command
/economy coinflip <amount> <heads/tails>
A simple 50/50 bet. You choose heads or tails and wager an amount of cash. If the coin lands on your chosen side, you double your money. If not, you lose your bet. There is no house edge — this is a true 50/50 chance. Great for quick high-risk plays. A cooldown applies between flips.
/economy coinflip 1000 heads → tails! lost 1,000 money
Pay Command
/economy pay @user <amount>
The pay command transfers money from your cash to another user's cash. This is how you send money to friends, reward server members, or make purchases in informal trades. You must have enough cash on hand (not banked) to send. There is a minimum amount and the recipient must have their balance initialized (used the bot at least once).
Deposit Command
/economy deposit <amount|all>
Deposit moves money from your cash into your bank for safe keeping. Once deposited, the money cannot be stolen by other users. Use all as the amount to deposit everything at once. Depositing is instant with no fees. This is the recommended action after earning a large amount of money, especially in servers where rob is active.
Withdraw Command
/economy withdraw <amount|all>
Withdraw moves money from your bank back into your cash. You need cash (not banked money) to buy items, gamble, or pay others. Use all to withdraw everything at once. There are no fees for withdrawing. Remember that withdrawn money is now exposed to robbery.
Economy Leaderboard Command
/leaderboard economy
The economy leaderboard shows the richest users in the server ranked by their total net worth (cash + bank combined). It is a great way to see who has been playing the most or who has the best investment strategy. The leaderboard is server-specific — only members of the current server are shown.

LEVELING SYSTEM

8 terms
XP (Experience Points) Concept
Earned by chatting in servers
XP (Experience Points) is the currency of the leveling system. You earn XP automatically by sending messages in any server where Astra is present. Each message earns a small random amount of XP. There is an XP cooldown to prevent spam abuse — only one batch of XP is granted per message per cooldown window. XP accumulates permanently; it never resets (unless the server admin resets the leaderboard).
See also Level XP Cooldown
Level Concept
Your progression milestone
Your level represents how active you are in a server. As you accumulate XP, you automatically advance to higher levels. The XP required per level increases on a balanced curve — early levels are fast to reach, while higher levels require significantly more activity. Each level-up triggers an announcement in the server (if configured). Some servers give special roles as rewards for reaching certain levels.
/leveling rank → Level 15 | 4,200 / 5,000 XP to next level
Rank Card Command
/leveling rank [@user]
The rank card displays your current level, total XP, and a progress bar showing how far you are toward the next level. You can view your own rank or check another user's by mentioning them. The rank card also shows your position on the server leaderboard. It is the primary way to track your leveling progress.
Level Leaderboard Command
/leaderboard levels
The level leaderboard ranks all server members from highest to lowest XP. It shows each user's level, total XP, and relative position. The leaderboard is server-specific — your level in one server does not affect your ranking in another. Compete with others by staying active to climb to the top.
XP Cooldown Concept
Anti-spam protection for XP
The XP cooldown is a built-in rate limit that prevents users from earning XP by spamming messages. After earning XP from a message, there is a short waiting period (typically ~60 seconds) before the next message can earn XP again. This ensures that XP represents genuine engagement rather than spam. Messages sent during the cooldown window still send normally — they just do not award XP.
Role Reward Concept
Automatic role at a set level
Role rewards are Discord roles that are automatically assigned to a user when they reach a specific level. Server administrators configure which roles are given at which levels using the leveling config commands. Role rewards are a great way to recognize active members with special perks — for example, giving a "Regular" role at level 10 or "Veteran" at level 50. Roles are assigned instantly when the level is reached.
Level-Up Announcement Concept
Public notification on level up
When a user reaches a new level, Astra automatically sends a level-up announcement in the server. This message congratulates the user, shows their new level, and tags them so they see the notification. Server admins can configure which channel announcements appear in, or disable them entirely. The announcement can also show any role rewards earned at that level.
Leveling Config Command
/leveling_config
The leveling config command is for server administrators only. It allows you to customize how the leveling system works in your server: set the announcement channel, toggle level-up messages on or off, add role rewards for specific levels, and remove existing role rewards. Changes take effect immediately. Requires server administrator permissions.

MODERATION

9 terms
Ban Command
/moderation ban @user [reason]
Banning permanently removes a user from the server and prevents them from rejoining via any invite. The banned user is automatically DMed with the reason (if Astra can reach them). The action is logged in the mod log channel with a case number, moderator name, target user, reason, and timestamp. Bans can be undone with an unban command. Requires the Ban Members Discord permission.
See also Case DM Warning
Kick Command
/moderation kick @user [reason]
Kicking removes a user from the server, but unlike a ban, they can rejoin with a valid invite link. It is a softer action — suitable for rule violations that do not warrant a permanent ban. The user receives a DM notification with the reason. The action is logged as a mod case. Requires the Kick Members Discord permission.
Warn Command
/moderation warn @user [reason]
A warning is a formal notice given to a user for breaking server rules. The user stays in the server and can continue chatting, but the warning is recorded in Astra's mod history as a case. The warned user receives a DM letting them know they were warned, who warned them, and why. Staff can view a user's full warning history to track repeat offenders. Requires the Moderate Members or Kick Members permission.
Timeout Command
/moderation timeout @user <duration> [reason]
A timeout (also called a mute) temporarily prevents a user from sending messages, adding reactions, or joining voice channels for a set duration. It uses Discord's native timeout feature, so it works reliably even without special permission setups. After the duration expires, the user is automatically unmuted. Timeouts are logged as mod cases. Duration can be specified in minutes, hours, or days.
/moderation timeout @bob 1h spamming → times out for 1 hour
Purge Command
/moderation purge <1-100>
Purge bulk-deletes a specified number of recent messages from the current channel (up to 100 at once). This is useful for cleaning up spam, inappropriate content, or test messages. Note: Discord only allows bulk deletion of messages up to 14 days old — older messages must be deleted one by one. Requires the Manage Messages permission. The deleted message count is confirmed in a temporary reply.
Case Concept
Logged moderation record
Every moderation action (warn, kick, ban, timeout) creates a case — a permanent logged record of the action. Each case has a unique number and stores: the action type, the target user, the moderator who performed it, the reason given, and the exact timestamp. Cases can be viewed per-user to see their full moderation history. This helps track repeat offenders and provides accountability for staff actions.
DM Warning Concept
Automatic user notification
When a moderation action is taken against a user (warn, kick, ban, timeout), Astra automatically attempts to send them a direct message explaining what happened. The DM includes the action taken, the reason provided by the moderator, and the server name. If the user has DMs disabled or has blocked the bot, the action still completes — the DM failure is noted in the confirmation message to the moderator.
Role Protection Concept
Prevent moderating protected roles
Role protection is a safety feature that prevents Astra from performing moderation actions on users who hold certain high-privilege roles (such as server admins or other moderators). This prevents junior staff from accidentally banning senior staff, and protects server ownership hierarchies. The protection is based on Discord's built-in role hierarchy — users with roles above the bot's role cannot be moderated through Astra.
Mod Log Channel Concept
Staff audit trail channel
The mod log channel is a designated Discord channel where Astra posts a detailed embed for every moderation action taken in the server. It creates a transparent audit trail visible to all staff. Server admins can configure which channel to use via /server_setup. The channel should be staff-only to keep moderation records private from regular members.

AI ASSISTANT

6 terms
AI Assistant Concept
Built-in AI chatbot
The AI Assistant is Astra's built-in conversational AI, powered by Google Gemini. You can ask it questions, have natural conversations, get help with homework, brainstorm ideas, and much more. The AI is available 24/7 with no rate limits. It works in server channels via /ai chat and in direct messages by simply sending Astra any message — no command needed in DMs.
See also Gemini DM AI
Gemini Concept
Google's AI model powering Astra AI
Gemini is the family of large language models (LLMs) built by Google. Astra uses the free tier of Gemini to power its AI assistant feature. Gemini models are capable of understanding and generating natural language, answering questions across a wide range of topics, summarizing text, writing code, and engaging in multi-turn conversations. The free models are used so the AI feature is available to all users at no cost.
AI Model Concept
The AI "brain" being used
Astra supports multiple Gemini model variants. Different models have different strengths — some are faster, some are smarter, and some are better at specific tasks. You can switch between available free models using the /ai model command. Your model preference is saved per-user. If you do not set a model, Astra uses the default recommended model.
/ai model → opens model selection menu
DM AI Concept
AI in your direct messages
One of Astra's most unique features is that the AI works in direct messages without any commands. Simply open a DM with Astra and send any message — the AI responds automatically. This makes Astra function as a personal AI assistant you can access from anywhere in Discord. No server is required; just you and the bot.
/ai chat Command
/ai chat <message>
The /ai chat command lets you use the AI assistant in server channels rather than DMs. Type your question or message as the prompt argument and Astra will reply with the AI's response in an embed. The response is visible to everyone in the channel. For private conversations, prefer DMing the bot directly.
Context Concept
The AI's conversation memory
Context refers to the conversation history the AI uses to understand follow-up messages. In Astra's DM AI mode, the AI maintains context across your conversation in a session, allowing you to ask follow-up questions naturally. For example, after asking "What is a black hole?" you can follow up with "How big are they?" and the AI understands you mean black holes. Context may reset between new sessions.

STOCK MARKET

7 terms
Stock Market Concept
Virtual investment simulation
The Astra Stock Market is a simulated real-time stock trading system. Five fictional companies trade at dynamic prices that move up and down over time, mimicking real market behavior. You use your in-bot money (from the economy system) to buy and sell shares. The goal is to buy low and sell high to grow your wealth. The market runs continuously — prices change whether you are online or not.
Stock Concept
A tradable virtual company share
A stock represents a share in one of the five fictional companies on the Astra market. Each stock has a ticker symbol, a current price, and a price history. Prices fluctuate based on the simulation algorithm. There is no real money involved — all values are virtual economy money. Buying a stock means purchasing one or more shares at the current market price.
Buy Command
/stockmarket buy <ticker> <shares>
The buy command purchases a specified number of shares in a stock at the current market price. The total cost (price × shares) is deducted from your cash balance. You must have sufficient cash to complete the purchase. Shares are added to your portfolio immediately at the purchase price, which is used later to calculate your profit or loss.
/stockmarket buy NOVA 10 → bought 10 shares at 450 each (cost: 4,500)
Sell Command
/stockmarket sell <ticker> <shares>
The sell command sells a specified number of shares you own at the current market price. The proceeds (price × shares) are added to your cash balance. If the current price is higher than your purchase price, you make a profit. If it is lower, you take a loss. You cannot sell more shares than you own. Shares are removed from your portfolio on sale.
Portfolio Concept
/stockmarket portfolio
Your portfolio is a summary of all the stocks you currently own. It shows each stock's ticker, the number of shares held, your average purchase price, the current market price, and your unrealized profit or loss for each position. The portfolio command gives you a full snapshot of your investment performance. A smart trader checks their portfolio regularly to decide when to sell.
Price Curve Concept
How a stock's price moves over time
Each stock follows a price curve — a pattern of price movement driven by the simulation algorithm. Prices can trend upward (bull run), downward (bear run), or fluctuate sideways. The algorithm uses randomized but realistic market mechanics including volatility, trend momentum, and mean reversion. No stock is guaranteed to rise or fall — studying patterns gives a trading edge.
Market View Command
/stockmarket market
The market view command shows the current prices, 24-hour price changes, and trend indicators for all five tradable stocks. Use it to spot opportunities — stocks showing strong upward trends may be worth buying, while those falling sharply might be worth selling. The market updates in real time as the simulation progresses.

GIVEAWAY ENGINE

6 terms
Giveaway Concept
/giveaway start
A giveaway is a community contest where one or more winners are randomly selected from everyone who entered. Giveaways run for a set time period. Members enter by clicking a reaction button on the giveaway message. When the timer expires, Astra picks the winners automatically and announces them. Giveaways can be for anything — Discord Nitro, in-game items, server roles, or any other prize the server offers.
Draw Concept
The winner selection moment
The draw is the moment when Astra randomly selects winner(s) from all entries. It happens automatically when the giveaway timer runs out, or can be triggered early with /giveaway end. The draw uses a cryptographically random selection so every entrant has an equal and fair chance. The winners are announced in the same channel as the original giveaway message.
Winner Concept
User(s) selected in the draw
A winner is any user who was randomly selected during the giveaway draw. Up to 10 winners can be selected in a single giveaway (configured when creating it). Each winner is mentioned in the announcement message and congratulated. If a winner is found to be ineligible or is a bot account, the giveaway can be rerolled to select a new winner.
Reroll Command
/giveaway reroll <message-id>
Reroll picks a brand new winner for an already-ended giveaway. This is useful if the original winner did not claim their prize, was found to be a bot/alt account, or otherwise should not receive the prize. The reroll selects a new user from the original entry pool (excluding the previous winner). Only staff with the Manage Server permission can use this command.
Entry Concept
Participation in a giveaway
An entry is a single user's participation in a giveaway. Members enter by clicking the 🎉 reaction button on the giveaway embed. Each user can only enter once per giveaway. You can un-enter by removing your reaction. Bot accounts are automatically filtered out from the entry list to ensure only real users can win.
Duration Concept
How long the giveaway runs
The duration is how long the giveaway stays open for entries before the draw happens. Astra supports durations from 1 minute up to 7 days. You specify the duration when creating the giveaway using time notation (e.g., 1h, 30m, 2d). The giveaway embed shows a live countdown to the end time. Longer giveaways attract more entries and build more excitement in the community.

SUPPORT TICKETS

5 terms
Ticket Concept
/ticket open
A ticket is a private channel automatically created for a specific user to communicate with the server's staff. Only the user who opened the ticket and staff members (users with the configured staff role) can see and interact in the channel. Tickets are commonly used for ban appeals, reports, rule questions, or any issue that needs private one-on-one attention. Each user can only have one open ticket at a time.
Transcript Concept
Saved ticket conversation log
A transcript is a saved copy of all messages exchanged in a ticket channel. When a ticket is closed, Astra can optionally save the entire conversation as a transcript sent to a designated log channel. Transcripts preserve the full context of what was discussed for future reference — useful for reviewing staff conduct, tracking recurring issues, or keeping evidence of rule violations.
Staff Role Concept
Role with ticket access
The staff role is the Discord role configured in Astra's server setup that grants access to all ticket channels. Any member with this role can view, read, and respond in every active ticket. When a new ticket is created, Astra automatically gives the staff role permission to see the channel. This is set up via /server_setup.
Close Command
/ticket close
Closing a ticket ends the support session and deletes the private channel (after saving a transcript if configured). Either the user or any staff member can close a ticket. A confirmation step prevents accidental closures. After closing, the user can open a new ticket if they need further help. The closed ticket is logged to the ticket-log channel with a summary.
Add / Remove Command
/ticket add @user · /ticket remove @user
Staff can add or remove specific users from an existing ticket channel. This is useful for escalating a ticket to a senior moderator, adding a second staff member for complex issues, or removing someone who was accidentally granted access. The targeted user immediately gains or loses visibility of the ticket channel.

WELCOME SYSTEM

5 terms
Welcome Message Concept
Auto greeting when someone joins
A welcome message is automatically sent to a designated channel whenever a new member joins the server. The message is fully customizable using template variables like {user} (mentions the new member), {server} (server name), and {membercount} (current member count). This creates a warm, personalized first impression for every new joiner. Configured via /welcome setup.
Welcome {user} to {server}! You're member #{membercount}!
Farewell Message Concept
Auto message when someone leaves
Similar to welcome messages, farewell messages are sent when a member leaves (or is kicked/banned from) the server. They use the same template variable system. Farewell messages are optional and can be enabled or disabled independently of welcome messages. They appear in the same configured welcome channel.
Auto-Role Concept
Role given automatically on join
Auto-role automatically assigns a specified Discord role to every new member who joins the server. This is commonly used to give new members a "Member" role that grants basic channel access, or a "New Member" role for verification flows. The role is assigned within seconds of joining. If the role assignment fails (e.g., the role is above Astra's role in the hierarchy), Astra logs the error. Configured in /welcome setup.
Welcome Channel Concept
Channel where greetings appear
The welcome channel is the Discord text channel where welcome and farewell messages are sent. Typically this is a public channel like #welcome or #general so new members are seen by the whole community. You set the welcome channel when configuring the welcome system. Astra must have permission to send messages in that channel.
Welcome Test Command
/welcome test
The test command triggers a mock welcome message using your own account as the "new member." This lets you preview exactly how your welcome message will look — including how template variables are replaced — before a real member joins. It is highly recommended to run a test after configuring the welcome system to catch any formatting errors.

SERVER SHOP

7 terms
Shop Concept
/shop view
The shop is a per-server virtual store where members can spend their economy money to purchase items and roles. Each server has its own unique shop configured by the server's administrators. Items can grant roles, provide passive income, give cosmetic badges, or be purely consumable. The shop encourages members to stay active and earn money to spend on meaningful rewards.
Item Concept
Something buyable in the shop
An item is any purchasable object in the server shop. Items have a name, description, price, type (role, consumable, passive income), and optionally a quantity limit. When you buy an item, it is added to your inventory and any associated effects (like role grants or passive income) are activated. Admins can add, edit, and remove items using the shop admin commands.
Role Item Concept
Shop item that grants a Discord role
A role item is a shop item that automatically grants the buyer a specific Discord role upon purchase. This is useful for creating paid ranks, exclusive access roles, or cosmetic vanity titles that members can earn through economy activity. The role is assigned instantly when the purchase is confirmed. Admins create role items by linking a shop item to a Discord role during setup.
Consumable Concept
Single-use item
A consumable is an item that can only be used once and is removed from your inventory after use. Examples include energy drinks (that temporarily boost XP gain), boost tokens, or event items. Consumables add a one-time benefit and encourage members to keep buying from the shop. They are distinct from role items (permanent) and passive income items (ongoing).
Passive Income Item Concept
Item that generates recurring money
Passive income items are shop purchases that generate money over time, collectible via /economy harvest. Examples include farms, mines, or businesses. Once purchased and added to your inventory, they silently accumulate money in the background. The more of these items you own, the more your harvest yields. They represent a long-term investment strategy in the economy.
Inventory Command
/shop inventory
Your inventory is a list of all items you currently own from the server shop. It shows the item name, type, quantity, and any associated effects. Consumable items show how many uses remain. Passive income items show their per-hour income rate. Role items show whether the role is currently applied. Use this command to track what you have purchased.
Sell Back Command
/shop sell <item>
You can sell items back to the shop for 50% of their original price. This gives you a partial refund if you no longer want an item. For role items, selling back removes the associated role as well. The refund is added directly to your cash balance. You cannot sell items for more than you paid — the sell-back price is always exactly half the purchase price.

DISCORD TERMS

8 terms
Slash Command Concept
Commands starting with /
Slash commands are the primary way to interact with Astra. Type / in any Discord chat to see a menu of all available commands with descriptions and parameter hints. Slash commands are native Discord interactions — they are registered directly with Discord and work in any server Astra is in, as well as in DMs. They provide autocomplete, built-in validation, and a clean user interface.
Prefix Command Concept
Commands starting with -
Prefix commands use a hyphen - as a prefix (e.g., -daily, -balance). They are primarily available in DMs since slash commands require a server context. Prefix commands are a legacy style but are maintained for DM accessibility. Type -help in a DM with Astra to see the full list of DM prefix commands.
Embed Concept
Formatted rich message box
An embed is a specially formatted message box that Discord bots can send. Astra uses embeds for all its responses — they have a colored left border (Astra's brand color), a title, description, fields, and sometimes a footer. Embeds make bot responses easy to read and visually distinct from regular chat messages. If Astra lacks the Embed Links permission, its responses will be plain text instead.
Server (Guild) Concept
A Discord community/guild
In Discord's technical API, a "server" is called a "guild." Astra uses both terms interchangeably. A server is a collection of channels, members, and roles managed by its owner and admins. Astra's features like the shop, leveling leaderboard, giveaways, and tickets are all scoped to individual servers. Your economy balance, however, is global across all servers.
Permission Concept
What a user or bot is allowed to do
Permissions control what actions users and bots can perform in a Discord server. Astra requires specific permissions to operate — for example, Manage Messages for purge, Kick Members for kick, and Ban Members for ban. If Astra is missing a required permission, the command will fail and tell you what is needed. When adding Astra to a server, it is recommended to use the official invite link which requests all necessary permissions upfront.
Role Concept
A labeled group with permissions
A Discord role is a named label assigned to members that can grant or restrict permissions, give a color in chat, and group members visibly in the member list. Astra interacts heavily with roles — granting level reward roles, shop role items, auto-roles for new members, and checking staff roles for ticket access. Role positions in the hierarchy matter: Astra can only assign and manage roles that are positioned below its own highest role.
Channel Concept
A room within a Discord server
Channels are the rooms within a Discord server where conversations happen. Text channels support messages, embeds, and commands. Astra creates ticket channels dynamically (one per open ticket) and posts announcements to configured channels (mod log, welcome channel, ticket log). Astra needs View Channel, Send Messages, and Embed Links permissions in any channel it needs to use.
DM (Direct Message) Concept
Private chat between you and Astra
A DM (Direct Message) is a private one-on-one conversation between a user and Astra that exists outside any server. In Astra's DM mode, the bot supports economy prefix commands (-daily, -balance, etc.) and the AI assistant responds automatically to any message. This makes Astra useful even when you are not in a server. To start a DM, click on Astra's profile and select "Message."

BOT & TECH TERMS

7 terms
Shard / Sharding Concept
How Astra scales to many servers
Sharding is the process of splitting a large Discord bot into multiple processes (shards) so it can handle more servers than a single process allows. Discord requires bots in 2,500+ servers to shard. Astra uses an automatic sharding manager — it launches the correct number of shards based on server count. Each shard handles a subset of servers. Users never notice sharding; it is transparent. Shard 0 also handles global command synchronization.
User App Integration Concept
Add Astra to your profile (not a server)
Discord's "User Apps" feature lets you install Astra directly to your profile rather than a server. Once installed, you can use Astra commands in any server you are in — even servers where Astra has not been added. The "Add to Profile" button installs Astra as a user app. This is a different install path from the traditional bot add flow and enables broader personal use.
Latency / Ping Command
/system ping
Latency (ping) measures how long it takes for Astra to communicate with Discord's servers. It is reported in milliseconds (ms). A lower number is better. /system ping shows both the bot's WebSocket latency (the heartbeat connection) and API round-trip time (how long a command takes to complete). High ping (>500ms) may indicate network issues or high server load.
/system ping → WebSocket: 48ms | API: 124ms
Uptime Command
/system status
Uptime is how long Astra has been continuously running without a restart or crash. The /system status command shows the current uptime, memory usage, CPU load, and shard count. A high uptime indicates a stable, healthy bot. Astra is hosted on Railway for reliable 24/7 operation. Planned restarts (for updates) are the most common cause of uptime resets.
Database Concept
Where all user data is stored
Astra stores all persistent data (user balances, levels, mod cases, shop inventories, giveaway entries, etc.) in a database. In development it uses SQLite (a local file); in production it uses PostgreSQL (a robust cloud database via Supabase). The database is the reason your economy balance, level, and mod history persist across restarts and server changes. Data is never stored in memory alone.
Server Setup Command
/server_setup
The server setup command is a configuration wizard for server administrators. It lets you configure all of Astra's per-server settings in one place: set the mod log channel, configure the ticket staff role and category, set the welcome channel and message, and more. It requires Administrator permission in the server. A full reset option is also available to clear all server-specific settings.
